### **Day 2 (Tuesday): Deep Dive on Finalists**

**Morning Session (2 hours)**
- **Topic:** Feasibility Deep Dive
- **For each of your top 3 ideas:**

  **Technical Feasibility:**
  - Can you build with existing LLMs (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.)?
  - Do you need special models/APIs?
  - What's the technical complexity?
  - Can you demonstrate it working?

  **Data Feasibility:**
  - What data do you need?
  - Can you access it? (public datasets, your own data, synthetic)
  - How will you handle privacy/legal?
  - Quality sufficient?

  **Time Feasibility:**
  - Can you complete in 3 weeks (Weeks 10-11 + buffer)?
  - What's the minimum viable version?
  - What can be mocked/simulated?

**Evening Session (2 hours)**
- **Topic:** Market & Interview Validation
- **For each idea:**

  **Market Validation:**
  - Does this solve a real problem people have?
  - Who are the competitors?
  - What's your unique angle?
  - Is this defensible/interesting?

  **Interview Story:**
  - Can you explain the problem clearly?
  - Is the solution compelling?
  - Does it demonstrate strategic thinking?
  - Will interviewers understand it?

**Activity:**
- Write 1-page brief for each of the 3 ideas
- Get feedback from 2-3 people
- **Select Final Idea** by end of day

### **Day 3 (Wednesday): Opportunity Assessment**

**Morning Session (2 hours)**
- **Topic:** Problem Definition & User Research
- **For your selected idea:**

  1. **Problem Statement**
     - What is the user struggle?
     - How big is the problem?
     - How do users solve it today?
     - Why do current solutions fail?

  2. **Target User**
     - Who specifically?
     - What's their context?
     - What's their technical proficiency?
     - What do they value?

  3. **User Research** (if possible)
     - Interview 3-5 potential users
     - Validate the problem
     - Understand current workflows
     - Test initial concepts

**Evening Session (2 hours)**
- **Topic:** Solution Definition
- **Define:**

  1. **Core Solution:**
     - How does AI solve the problem?
     - What's the key insight/innovation?
     - Why is this AI-native (vs. traditional software)?

  2. **Value Proposition:**
     - What's the key benefit?
     - How much better than alternatives?
     - What's the "wow" moment?

  3. **AI Approach:**
     - What type of AI? (LLM, RAG, classification, etc.)
     - Which model(s)?
     - What's the architecture?

**Deliverable Start:** Opportunity assessment document

---

### **Day 4 (Thursday): Scope & Success Criteria**

**Morning Session (2 hours)**
- **Topic:** Define MVP Scope
- **Activity:**

  1. **Full Vision:** What's the dream version?
  2. **Phase 1 (MVP):** What's the minimum to demonstrate value?
  3. **Phase 2:** What comes next?
  4. **Out of Scope:** What are you explicitly NOT building?

  **MVP Definition Criteria:**
  - Can be built in 2 weeks
  - Demonstrates core value prop
  - Shows AI PM skills
  - Can be demoed in interview
  - Has clear success metrics

**Evening Session (2 hours)**
- **Topic:** Success Metrics & Evaluation
- **Define:**

  1. **North Star Metric:**
     - What's the one metric that matters most?

  2. **Product Metrics:**
     - How do you measure user value?
     - What indicates success?

  3. **ML Metrics:**
     - How do you measure AI quality?
     - What's acceptable performance?

  4. **Success Criteria:**
     - What does "good enough" look like?
     - How will you know it's working?

**Deliverable:** MVP scope + success criteria

## üí° What Makes a Great Portfolio Project?

**‚úÖ Great Portfolio Projects:**
- Solve a real problem you've experienced
- Show AI-native thinking (couldn't be built without AI)
- Demonstrate full PM skill stack
- Have a compelling "why now" story
- Can be prototyped quickly
- Unique angle or innovation
- Impressive in interviews

**‚ùå Weak Portfolio Projects:**
- Generic chatbot
- Solved problem without unique insight
- Too simple (doesn't show skills)
- Too complex (can't finish)
- No clear user or value prop
- Boring/overdone

## üéì Interview Application

### **You'll be asked: "Tell me about a product you built"**

**Your Answer Structure:**
1. **Problem** (30 sec)
   - "I noticed that [user segment] struggles with [problem]"
   - "Current solutions fail because [why]"

2. **Solution** (45 sec)
   - "I built [product name], an AI-powered [category]"
   - "The key innovation is [unique insight]"
   - "It uses [AI approach] to [core value prop]"

3. **Process** (1 min)
   - "I started with discovery: [research insights]"
   - "Defined success metrics: [North Star]"
   - "Built and tested a prototype"
   - "Evaluated with [evaluation approach]"

4. **Results & Learnings** (30 sec)
   - "Achieved [metrics]"
   - "Key learning: [insight]"
   - "If I did it again: [what you'd change]"

**Practice this 3-minute pitch!**
