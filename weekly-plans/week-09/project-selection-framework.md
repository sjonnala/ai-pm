## Evaluation Criteria

### Criterion 1: Skills Showcase (Weight: 25%)

**Question:** Does this project demonstrate comprehensive AI PM skills?

**Skills to Demonstrate:**

| Skill | How to Show It |
|-------|----------------|
| **Strategic Thinking** | Clear problem framing, opportunity sizing, "why now" |
| **Discovery** | User research, validation, competitive analysis |
| **AI Product Design** | Model selection, architecture, AI-specific requirements |
| **Execution** | Working prototype, complete PRD, thoughtful decisions |
| **Metrics & Evaluation** | Comprehensive eval framework, quantitative results |
| **UX Design** | AI-native flows, human-in-loop, error handling |
| **GTM** | Positioning, pricing, launch strategy |
| **Technical Depth** | RAG, agents, multimodal, or other sophisticated AI |

**Scoring Guide:**

- **5:** Shows ALL key AI PM skills comprehensively
- **4:** Shows most skills with depth
- **3:** Shows core skills but missing some areas
- **2:** Only shows a few skills
- **1:** Doesn't demonstrate AI PM expertise

**Examples:**

- **Score 5:** "AI-powered code reviewer with RAG that learns from codebase patterns" - Shows architecture, evaluation, UX, technical depth
- **Score 3:** "Simple chatbot for customer support" - Shows basic AI integration but limited sophistication
- **Score 1:** "Wrapper around ChatGPT API" - No unique AI PM thinking

---

### Criterion 2: Feasibility (Weight: 25%)

**Question:** Can you realistically build this in 3 weeks with available resources?

**Factors to Consider:**

**Technical Complexity:**
- Can use existing LLM APIs (GPT-4, Claude, etc.)
- Frameworks available (LangChain, etc.)
- No custom ML training required
- Integrations are achievable

**Data Availability:**
- Data is accessible (public, personal, synthetic)
- Enough volume and quality
- No legal/privacy barriers

**Time Required:**
- 40-50 hours total is realistic
- Core features achievable
- Demo-able in 2 weeks

**Resource Constraints:**
- Budget < $100 for APIs/tools
- No specialized hardware needed
- Can use free/cheap tools

**Scoring Guide:**

- **5:** Highly confident can complete with time to spare
- **4:** Confident with good execution
- **3:** Feasible but will need to cut scope
- **2:** Very challenging, high risk
- **1:** Unrealistic in 3 weeks

**Examples:**

- **Score 5:** "RAG-based documentation chatbot" - Well-established patterns, clear implementation path
- **Score 3:** "Video analysis AI with custom ML model" - Requires training, more complex
- **Score 1:** "Fully autonomous coding agent" - Too complex for 3 weeks

---

### Criterion 3: Uniqueness (Weight: 20%)

**Question:** Is this creative, differentiated, and interesting?

**What Makes a Project Unique:**

**Novel Application:**
- Applies AI to underserved domain
- Combines AI capabilities in new way
- Solves problem in innovative manner

**Differentiation:**
- Not just "ChatGPT wrapper"
- Has unique angle or insight
- Goes beyond obvious solutions

**Personal Touch:**
- Connected to your background/interests
- Shows authentic passion
- Has a story behind it

**Scoring Guide:**

- **5:** Highly creative, I've never seen this before
- **4:** Fresh take on common problem
- **3:** Some unique elements
- **2:** Fairly generic but has differentiator
- **1:** Completely overdone/generic

**Examples:**

- **Score 5:** "AI fitness coach that analyzes form from video and gives real-time feedback" - Novel combination of computer vision + coaching
- **Score 3:** "Email summarizer with sentiment analysis" - Common concept but specific implementation angle
- **Score 1:** "Basic ChatGPT chatbot" - Overdone, no differentiation

---

### Criterion 4: Interview Appeal (Weight: 20%)

**Question:** Will this create a compelling story and generate good interview discussion?

**What Makes Good Interview Material:**

**Clear Narrative:**
- Problem is relatable and understandable
- Solution is elegant and logical
- Results are measurable

**Discussion Depth:**
- Technical decisions to discuss
- Trade-offs to explain
- Learnings to share
- "What would you do differently" has good answer

**Memorability:**
- Interviewer will remember it
- Stands out from other candidates
- Shows personality

**Versatility:**
- Can discuss in product sense interview
- Can discuss in technical interview
- Can discuss in strategy interview

**Scoring Guide:**

- **5:** Will definitely spark great discussion and be memorable
- **4:** Strong interview material
- **3:** Adequate for interviews
- **2:** Might not generate much discussion
- **1:** Boring or hard to explain quickly

**Examples:**

- **Score 5:** "AI that helps non-technical founders write technical PRDs" - Relatable, clear value, interesting problem
- **Score 3:** "AI-powered todo list" - Understandable but not very exciting
- **Score 1:** "Complex ML pipeline" - Hard to explain, not relatable

---

### Criterion 5: Personal Interest (Weight: 10%)

**Question:** Are you genuinely excited to build this?

**Why This Matters:**

**Motivation:**
- You'll need to work 40+ hours on this
- Excitement sustains you through challenges
- Passion shows in interviews

**Authenticity:**
- Genuine interest comes through
- Can speak passionately about it
- Story feels real, not manufactured

**Domain Knowledge:**
- Existing knowledge accelerates build
- Personal experience adds credibility
- Can speak to problem deeply

**Scoring Guide:**

- **5:** Extremely excited, can't wait to build
- **4:** Very interested
- **3:** Interested enough
- **2:** Mildly interested
- **1:** Not really excited

**Note:** This is the lowest-weighted criterion because sometimes the best portfolio project isn't your dream project - and that's okay! But some interest helps.

---

### Criterion 6: Completeness Potential (Weight: 10%)

**Question:** Can you create a COMPLETE portfolio piece (not just a prototype)?

**What "Complete" Means:**

**All Deliverables:**
- Working prototype (demo-able)
- Comprehensive PRD
- UX flows
- Evaluation framework
- GTM strategy
- Demo video
- Case study

**Polish Level:**
- Professional enough to show employers
- No major bugs or gaps
- Clear documentation

**End-to-End Story:**
- Shows full product lifecycle
- Problem ‚Üí Solution ‚Üí Results
- Complete narrative arc

**Scoring Guide:**

- **5:** Can definitely deliver all materials professionally
- **4:** Can deliver most materials well
- **3:** Can deliver core materials
- **2:** Some materials will be incomplete
- **1:** Will struggle to create complete portfolio

## Scoring Framework

### How to Score Your Project Ideas

**Step 1: List 5-10 Project Ideas**

Brainstorm without filtering. Include:
- Ideas from Week 1 opportunity assessment
- New ideas that emerged
- Variations on themes
- "Crazy" ideas

**Step 2: Score Each Idea on 6 Criteria**

Use scoring sheet below. Rate 1-5 for each criterion.

**Step 3: Calculate Weighted Score**

```
Total Score =
  (Skills Showcase √ó 0.25) +
  (Feasibility √ó 0.25) +
  (Uniqueness √ó 0.20) +
  (Interview Appeal √ó 0.20) +
  (Personal Interest √ó 0.10) +
  (Completeness √ó 0.10)
```

**Step 4: Rank and Select Top 3**

Focus deep-dive analysis on top scorers.

**Step 5: Make Final Decision**

Choose ONE project to build.

## Red Flags to Avoid

### üö© Red Flag 1: Too Generic

**Warning Signs:**
- "Just another chatbot"
- "Wrapper around ChatGPT"
- No unique angle
- Seen 100 times before

**Why It's Bad:**
- Won't stand out in interviews
- Doesn't show strategic thinking
- Looks like tutorial project

**Fix:**
- Add unique domain application
- Combine multiple AI capabilities
- Solve specific niche problem
- Add novel UX pattern

---

### üö© Red Flag 2: Too Complex

**Warning Signs:**
- Requires custom ML training
- Needs months of work
- Requires specialized hardware
- Multiple complex integrations

**Why It's Bad:**
- Won't finish in time
- High stress and risk
- May not have demo-able output
- Wastes 3 weeks

**Fix:**
- Simplify scope dramatically
- Use pre-trained models
- Mock complex parts
- Focus on one use case

---

### üö© Red Flag 3: Unclear Value

**Warning Signs:**
- Can't explain problem clearly
- Value proposition is fuzzy
- No clear target user
- Solution seeking problem

**Why It's Bad:**
- Hard to pitch in interviews
- No compelling narrative
- Doesn't show product sense
- Interviewers won't understand it

**Fix:**
- Start with clear problem
- Validate with user research
- Define specific value metric
- Clarify who benefits

---

### üö© Red Flag 4: Data Impossible

**Warning Signs:**
- Requires proprietary data
- Privacy/legal concerns
- Need expensive datasets
- Data doesn't exist

**Why It's Bad:**
- Can't build without data
- Legal risks
- Delays and blockers
- May not be realistic

**Fix:**
- Use public datasets
- Create synthetic data
- Find alternative data source
- Pivot to different problem

---

### üö© Red Flag 5: No AI Necessary

**Warning Signs:**
- Could be built with rules/logic
- AI is just feature, not core
- Traditional software would work
- "AI washing"

**Why It's Bad:**
- Doesn't showcase AI PM skills
- Misses the point
- Won't impress AI PM interviewers
- No unique AI thinking

**Fix:**
- Find truly AI-native problem
- Make AI central to value prop
- Choose problem AI uniquely solves
- Show why deterministic approach fails
