## Part 3: Design Principles

### 3.1 Visual Design Best Practices

**Consistency:**
- Use a consistent color palette (2-3 main colors)
- Use consistent fonts (1 for headers, 1 for body)
- Use consistent layout (margins, spacing)
- Use consistent icon style

**Clarity:**
- One main idea per slide
- Large, readable fonts (minimum 20pt for body, 36pt+ for titles)
- High contrast (dark text on light background or vice versa)
- Whitespace is your friend (don't cram)

**Visual Hierarchy:**
- Most important info = largest
- Use bold, color, size to guide attention
- Align elements (left, center, rightâ€”but be consistent)

**Professional Polish:**
- No clipart or cheesy stock photos
- Use high-quality images (or simple graphics)
- Proofread! No typos
- Export as PDF for sharing (maintains formatting)

## Part 4: Presentation Best Practices

### 4.1 Delivery Tips

**Before You Present:**

- **Rehearse:** Practice 3-5 times until smooth
- **Time yourself:** Stay within your target (10-12 min)
- **Anticipate questions:** Prepare answers to likely questions
- **Know your slides:** Don't just read them
- **Test tech:** Ensure slides work on presentation device

**During Presentation:**

**Voice:**
- Speak clearly and at moderate pace
- Project confidence (even if nervous)
- Vary your tone (don't be monotone)
- Pause for emphasis
- Avoid filler words (um, uh, like)

**Body Language:**
- Stand tall, shoulders back
- Make eye contact (if presenting live)
- Use hand gestures (but don't overdo it)
- Move intentionally (don't pace)
- Smile (shows enthusiasm)

**Slide Discipline:**
- Don't read slides word-for-word
- Expand on what's on screen
- Point to visuals (if presenting live)
- Advance slides at appropriate pace (not too fast, not too slow)

---

### 4.2 Storytelling Techniques

**Hook Them Early:**

Start with a relatable story or statistic:
- "Imagine spending 20% of your week on a task everyone hates..."
- "Last year, companies wasted $10 billion on..."
- "Every engineer I talked to said the same thing..."

**Use the Rule of Three:**

Group things in threes (easier to remember):
- "Three reasons this matters..."
- "I learned three key lessons..."
- "We focus on speed, quality, and ease of use."

**Show, Don't Tell:**

Instead of: "Our AI is fast"
Say: "From code to documentation in 15 seconds. Let me show you."

**Use Transitions:**

Connect slides with transition phrases:
- "Now that we understand the problem, let's look at the solution..."
- "You saw how it works. Now let's talk about why this approach..."
- "With that context, here's the impact..."

---

### 4.3 Handling Questions

**Types of Questions:**

**Clarification Questions:**
- **Question:** "How does the AI handle [specific edge case]?"
- **Response:** Answer directly, reference your eval framework if applicable

**Challenge Questions:**
- **Question:** "Why not just use [competitor]?"
- **Response:** Acknowledge the alternative, then explain your differentiation

**Hypothetical Questions:**
- **Question:** "What if [unlikely scenario]?"
- **Response:** "That's an interesting edge case. Here's how I'd think about it..."

**General Tips:**

- **Don't panic:** All questions are opportunities to showcase thinking
- **It's okay to say "I don't know":** Follow with "but here's how I'd figure it out"
- **Clarify if needed:** "Just to make sure I understand, you're asking about X?"
- **Keep answers concise:** 30-60 seconds max
- **Tie back to your work:** Reference slides, artifacts, decisions you made

---

### 4.4 Remote Presentation Tips

**If presenting over Zoom/video call:**

- **Test tech ahead of time:** Screen share, audio, video
- **Close unnecessary tabs/apps:** Only show your slides
- **Look at camera when speaking:** Not at screen (if you want eye contact)
- **Share screen, not just window:** So viewers can see full slides
- **Use presenter view:** See your notes, next slide
- **Mute notifications:** No Slack/email interruptions
- **Have backup:** PDF version ready in case tech fails
