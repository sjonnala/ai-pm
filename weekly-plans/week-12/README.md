## üóìÔ∏è Daily Breakdown

### **Day 1 (Monday): Product Sense Interviews**

**Morning Session (2-3 hours)**
- **Topic:** Product Sense Framework
- **Master the structure:**

  **Product Sense Question Types:**
  - Design a product (e.g., "Design an AI feature for Spotify")
  - Improve a product (e.g., "How would you improve Google Search with AI?")
  - Favorite product (e.g., "What's your favorite AI product and why?")
  - Product critique (e.g., "What do you think of ChatGPT?")

  **Framework (CIRCLES Method):**
  1. **C**larify - Ask questions about context, users, constraints
  2. **I**dentify - Define user and their needs
  3. **R**eport - State your approach
  4. **C**ut through - Prioritize
  5. **L**ist solutions - Brainstorm
  6. **E**valuate - Trade-offs
  7. **S**ummarize - Recap recommendation

  **AI-Specific Considerations:**
  - Why AI? (vs. traditional approach)
  - What ML approach?
  - Data availability?
  - Evaluation strategy?
  - Failure modes and guardrails?

- **Resources:**
  - üìö [Cracking the PM Interview](http://www.crackingthepminterview.com/) - Gayle McDowell
  - üìñ [Lenny's Newsletter - Interview Guides](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/)
  - üé• [Exponent - PM Interview Course](https://www.tryexponent.com/)

**Evening Session (2-3 hours)**
- **Activity:** Practice Product Sense Questions
- **Practice these:**
  1. "Design an AI writing assistant for students"
  2. "How would you add AI to Notion?"
  3. "What's your favorite AI product? How would you improve it?"
  4. "Design a feature that uses AI for [your favorite app]"

**For each:**
  - Write out full answer (using CIRCLES)
  - Time yourself (25-30 min per question)
  - Record yourself (audio/video)
  - Review and refine

**Deliverable Start:** Interview prep document

### **Day 2 (Tuesday): Execution & Strategy Interviews**

**Morning Session (2-3 hours)**
- **Topic:** Execution Interviews
- **Master execution questions:**

  **Execution Question Types:**
  - Metrics (e.g., "What metrics would you track for GitHub Copilot?")
  - Diagnosis (e.g., "DAU dropped 10%. How do you investigate?")
  - Feature prioritization (e.g., "You have 10 feature requests. How do you prioritize?")
  - Roadmap (e.g., "Build a roadmap for AI in Gmail")
  - Launch (e.g., "How would you launch an AI feature?")
  - A/B test (e.g., "How would you A/B test a recommendation algorithm?")

  **Framework for Execution:**
  1. Clarify the goal/metric
  2. Identify hypotheses
  3. Prioritize (impact vs. effort)
  4. Define success metrics
  5. Create action plan
  6. Consider risks
  7. Summarize

  **AI-Specific for Execution:**
  - ML metrics vs. product metrics
  - Drift monitoring
  - A/B testing challenges with AI
  - Cost considerations
  - Safety and compliance

**Evening Session (2-3 hours)**
- **Topic:** Strategy Interviews
- **Master strategy questions:**

  **Strategy Question Types:**
  - Market entry (e.g., "Should Google build an AI-first search competitor?")
  - Competitive strategy (e.g., "How should Notion compete with Microsoft Copilot?")
  - Pricing (e.g., "How would you price ChatGPT?")
  - Partnership (e.g., "Should Spotify partner with OpenAI?")
  - Portfolio (e.g., "What AI products should Amazon prioritize?")

  **Framework for Strategy:**
  1. Understand the business context
  2. Analyze market landscape
  3. Identify strategic options
  4. Evaluate trade-offs
  5. Make recommendation
  6. Define success criteria

**Activity:** Practice 4 execution + 2 strategy questions

### **Day 3 (Wednesday): Technical & Behavioral Interviews**

**Morning Session (2-3 hours)**
- **Topic:** Technical Interviews (for AI PM)**
- **What you need to know:**

  **Technical Knowledge Areas:**
  - ML basics (supervised, unsupervised, reinforcement learning)
  - Common algorithms (decision trees, neural networks, transformers)
  - Model evaluation (accuracy, precision, recall, F1, etc.)
  - ML lifecycle (data ‚Üí training ‚Üí deployment ‚Üí monitoring)
  - LLMs (how they work, prompting, fine-tuning, RAG)
  - System design (high-level architecture)
  - Data (collection, labeling, privacy, quality)
  - MLOps (deployment, monitoring, retraining)

  **Question Types:**
  - "Explain how a recommendation system works"
  - "What's the difference between precision and recall?"
  - "How does RAG work?"
  - "How would you design the ML system for [product]?"
  - "What factors affect LLM quality?"

  **Key:** You don't need to code, but you need to understand concepts and communicate with ML engineers

**Evening Session (2-3 hours)**
- **Topic:** Behavioral Interviews (STAR Method)
- **Master STAR Stories:**

  **STAR Format:**
  - **S**ituation - Set context
  - **T**ask - Your goal/challenge
  - **A**ction - What YOU did (specific actions)
  - **R**esult - Outcome (metrics if possible)

  **Common Behavioral Questions:**
  - Tell me about yourself
  - Why PM? Why AI PM?
  - Tell me about a time you failed
  - Tell me about a conflict with an engineer
  - Tell me about a time you influenced without authority
  - Tell me about a data-driven decision
  - Tell me about prioritizing competing requests
  - What's your biggest weakness?
  - Why our company?
  - Why are you interested in this role?

**Activity:** Draft 10+ STAR stories (use next section)

### **Day 5 (Friday): Mock Interviews**

**Morning Session (2-3 hours)**
- **Activity:** Self Mock Interview
- **Do full interviews solo:**
  1. Product Sense (30 min)
  2. Execution (30 min)
  3. Behavioral (30 min)

**Record yourself:**
  - Use phone or laptop camera
  - Treat it like real interview
  - Don't stop/restart
  - Answer as if talking to interviewer

**Review:**
  - Watch playback (painful but essential!)
  - Note:
    - Clarity of communication
    - Structure of answers
    - Confidence level
    - Filler words (um, uh, like)
    - Eye contact / body language
  - Identify 3 areas to improve

## üí° Interview Day Tips

**Before the Interview:**
- Research the company (products, news, culture)
- Review your frameworks
- Review your STAR stories
- Review your portfolio project
- Prepare questions to ask (3-5 thoughtful ones)
- Test your tech setup (for virtual interviews)
- Get good sleep!

**During the Interview:**
- Take a moment to think before answering
- Structure your answers (frameworks)
- Be conversational, not robotic
- Ask clarifying questions
- Think out loud
- Show enthusiasm
- Be honest about what you don't know
- Watch the time
- Take notes

**After the Interview:**
- Send thank you email within 24 hours
- Reflect on what went well and what to improve
- Follow up on any questions you couldn't answer
- Stay positive!

## üéØ Common Interview Questions & Frameworks

### Product Sense:
**Q:** "Design an AI feature for [product]"
**Framework:**
1. Clarify (users, goals, constraints)
2. Identify user needs
3. Prioritize opportunity
4. Brainstorm solutions
5. Evaluate (why AI? data? metrics?)
6. Recommend

### Execution:
**Q:** "What metrics for [AI product]?"
**Framework:**
- North Star metric
- Product metrics (AARRR)
- ML metrics (accuracy, latency, cost)
- Guardrails
- How they connect

### Strategy:
**Q:** "Should we build [AI product]?"
**Framework:**
1. Market analysis
2. Competitive landscape
3. Our strengths/weaknesses
4. Strategic options
5. Recommendation with rationale
6. Success metrics
7. Risks

### Behavioral:
**Q:** "Tell me about a time you failed"
**Framework:** STAR
- Specific failure
- What you learned
- How you applied learning
- Growth demonstrated
